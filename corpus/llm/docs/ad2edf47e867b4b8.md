**Beginner’s Tutorial: Using Barmwatch for Your Home Brewing Journey**

Welcome to the exciting world of home brewing! Whether you're just starting out or looking to refine your process, keeping track of your beer's fermentation journey is crucial. This tutorial will guide you through using the Barmwatch mobile app to log your batch, set temperature alerts, and export a comprehensive brew log as a PDF. By the end of this guide, you'll be able to monitor your beer’s fermentation with ease and confidence.

### Step 1: Downloading and Setting Up Barmwatch

First things first, download the Barmwatch app from either the Google Play Store or Apple App Store onto your smartphone. Once installed, open the app and create an account if you don’t already have one. A simple email registration will get you started.

### Step 2: Logging Your Brew Batch

1. **Create a New Batch Entry:** Tap on the “+” icon to add a new batch. Fill in essential details such as the name of your beer, type (e.g., IPA, Stout), and original gravity (OG) using a hydrometer or refractometer reading.

2. **Add Fermentation Details:** Enter the expected attenuation rate, which is the percentage by which sugars are converted into alcohol during fermentation. This helps predict your final gravity (FG).

3. **Set Up Additional Information:** Include notes about any special ingredients like hops varieties, yeast strains, and equipment used. These details can be invaluable for future reference or replication of successful batches.

### Step 3: Setting Temperature Alerts

Maintaining the right temperature is vital for a healthy fermentation process. Barmwatch allows you to set alerts based on your chosen yeast’s optimal temperature range:

1. **Navigate to Fermentation Settings:** After logging your batch, go to the fermentation section within the app. Here, find the temperature settings.

2. **Set Your Alerts:** Enter the minimum and maximum temperatures recommended for your specific yeast strain (usually found in the yeast packet). Choose how often you’d like to be alerted—daily, weekly, or custom intervals.

3. **Confirm Your Settings:** Once set, confirm that alerts will pop up on your phone if the temperature drifts outside the desired range. This feature ensures you can intervene promptly if needed.

### Step 4: Exporting a Brew Log as a PDF

After fermentation is complete and you’ve added any necessary notes or corrections, you might want to document everything for future reference:

1. **Access Your Batch Details:** Go back into your batch entry in Barmwatch. Tap on “Export” at the bottom of the screen.

2. **Choose PDF Format:** Select PDF as your export format. The app will compile all logged information—brew details, fermentation notes, temperature alerts, and any additional comments you’ve made.

3. **Save to Your Device:** Download the PDF directly onto your phone or tablet. You can also email it if needed for sharing with fellow brewers or archiving purposes.

### Additional Tips

- **Regular Updates:** Keep your app updated to enjoy new features and improved stability.
- **Backup Data:** Consider backing up important data from Barmwatch regularly, especially if you’re using multiple devices.
- **Explore Features:** Dive into the app’s help section for more advanced functionalities like creating recipes or setting up alerts for secondary fermentation phases.

By following these steps, you’ll be well on your way to mastering your home brewing process with the aid of Barmwatch. Happy brewing!
