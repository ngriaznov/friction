**MoltenPath Troubleshooting Guide**

Welcome to the MoltenPath troubleshooting guide, designed to help you resolve common issues that may arise while using your home 3D printer firmware. In this guide, we'll cover three critical problems: layer shifting mid-print, nozzle clogs, and bed adhesion failures. Follow these diagnostic steps and fixes to get your prints back on track.

**Problem 1: Layer Shifting Mid-Print**

Layer shifting occurs when the print head deviates from its intended path during a print job, resulting in distorted or incomplete layers. This can be caused by various factors, including loose belts, misaligned axes, or faulty stepper motor drivers.

### Diagnostic Steps:

1. **Check belt tension**: Ensure that the X, Y, and Z axis belts are properly tightened. Loose belts can cause layer shifting.
2. **Verify axis alignment**: Check if the print bed is square with the printer's frame and if the axes are properly aligned.
3. **Inspect stepper motor drivers**: Look for any signs of overheating or damage on the stepper motor drivers.

### Fixes:

1. **Tighten belts**: If the belts are loose, tighten them according to the manufacturer's instructions.
2. **Adjust axis alignment**: Use a caliper or a precision measuring tool to ensure the print bed is square with the frame and axes are aligned.
3. **Replace stepper motor drivers**: If you've identified faulty drivers, replace them with new ones.

**Problem 2: Nozzle Clogs**

Nozzle clogs occur when filament accumulates within the nozzle, preventing the printer from extruding material correctly. This can be caused by using incompatible filaments, poor nozzle maintenance, or clogged hotends.

### Diagnostic Steps:

1. **Check filament compatibility**: Ensure that you're using compatible filaments for your printer's temperature range.
2. **Inspect the nozzle**: Look for any signs of clogging or blockages within the nozzle.
3. **Clean the hotend**: Use a cleaning brush or a solvent to clean the hotend and remove any accumulated debris.

### Fixes:

1. **Switch to compatible filaments**: Change your filament to one that's suitable for your printer's temperature range.
2. **Clean the nozzle**: Use a cleaning brush or a solvent to clear any blockages within the nozzle.
3. **Replace the nozzle**: If the nozzle is severely clogged, replace it with a new one.

**Problem 3: Bed Adhesion Failures**

Bed adhesion failures occur when the print fails to adhere properly to the print bed, resulting in prints lifting or detaching mid-print. This can be caused by poor bed leveling, incorrect temperature settings, or using an incompatible bed surface material.

### Diagnostic Steps:

1. **Check bed leveling**: Ensure that the print bed is level and evenly spaced with the printer's frame.
2. **Verify temperature settings**: Check if the temperature settings for the extruder and hotbed are correct.
3. **Inspect the bed surface**: Look for any signs of debris or contamination on the bed surface.

### Fixes:

1. **Adjust bed leveling**: Use a caliper or a precision measuring tool to adjust the bed leveling screws.
2. **Adjust temperature settings**: Check if the temperature settings need adjustment and make necessary changes.
3. **Replace the bed surface material**: If you're using a non-stick coating, try replacing it with a more adhesive-friendly material.

By following these diagnostic steps and fixes, you should be able to resolve common issues that may arise while using MoltenPath firmware on your home 3D printer. Remember to always follow safety guidelines when working with electrical components and hotends. Happy printing!
