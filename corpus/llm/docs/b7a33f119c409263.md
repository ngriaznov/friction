Welcome to the world of home brewing! As you embark on this exciting journey, it's essential to keep track of your brews' progress. That's where Barmwatch comes in – a user-friendly mobile app designed specifically for brewers like you. In this tutorial, we'll guide you through logging a batch of beer, setting temperature alerts, and exporting a comprehensive brew log as a PDF.

**Downloading and Setting Up Barmwatch**

Before we dive into the nitty-gritty, make sure to download the Barmwatch app from the App Store (for iOS devices) or Google Play Store (for Android devices). Once installed, create an account by tapping on "Sign up" and follow the in-app instructions. You'll be asked to provide basic information like name, email, and password.

**Logging a Batch of Beer**

To log your first batch, follow these steps:

1. Tap on the "+" icon at the top right corner of the screen to create a new brew entry.
2. Fill in the required details:
	* **Brew Name**: Give your beer a unique name (e.g., "Hoppy IPA").
	* **Batch Size**: Enter the total volume of your brew (in liters or gallons).
	* **Style**: Choose from the pre-defined styles or create a custom entry.
3. Tap on "Save" to save your new brew.

**Tracking Fermentation**

As you monitor your beer's fermentation progress, Barmwatch will help you stay on top of it:

1. Log temperature readings: Enter the current temperature in the app, and Barmwatch will store this data for future reference.
2. Set temperature alerts: Tap on "Temperature" and set a target range (e.g., 20-25°C) to receive notifications when your brew reaches or exceeds these temperatures.

**Exporting Your Brew Log**

After completing fermentation, you can export a detailed log of your brewing process as a PDF:

1. Navigate to the home screen and select the logged batch.
2. Tap on "Details" (represented by three horizontal lines).
3. Scroll down to the "Actions" section and tap on "Export Brew Log."
4. Choose the desired output format: PDF or CSV.

**Tips for Exporting Your Brew Log**

* To ensure accuracy, make sure all temperature readings are logged in a timely manner.
* Barmwatch allows you to add notes and photos to your brew log. Take advantage of this feature to document any notable events or observations throughout fermentation.
* Consider setting reminders for regular temperature checks and other critical tasks.

**Conclusion**

Congratulations! You're now equipped with the knowledge to effectively use Barmwatch to track your brewing progress, monitor temperatures, and export a comprehensive brew log as a PDF. As you continue on this homebrewing journey, Barmwatch will become an invaluable tool in helping you refine your techniques, experiment with new recipes, and share your brewing successes with fellow enthusiasts.

**Troubleshooting Tips**

If you encounter any issues while using the app, refer to Barmwatch's official documentation or contact their support team. Common problems include:

* Forgotten passwords: Tap on "Forgot Password" and follow the in-app instructions.
* Inconsistent temperature readings: Double-check your thermometer calibration and ensure accurate logging.

**Final Thoughts**

Brewing is a journey of experimentation and learning, and Barmwatch is here to help you navigate it with ease. With this beginner's guide, you'll be well on your way to mastering the art of fermentation tracking. Happy brewing!
