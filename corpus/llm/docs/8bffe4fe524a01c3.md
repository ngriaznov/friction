Welcome to the world of home brewing! As a beginner, tracking your brewing process is crucial to ensure a successful batch of beer. In this tutorial, we'll guide you through using Barmwatch, a mobile app designed specifically for fermentation-tracking and brew logging.

**Downloading and Setting Up Barmwatch**

To get started, download the Barmwatch app from the App Store (for iOS devices) or Google Play Store (for Android devices). Once installed, follow these steps:

1. Launch the app and tap "Create New Brew" to start a new log.
2. Enter your brew details, including batch number, style, and ingredients.
3. Tap "Add Fermenter" to create a new entry for your fermenter. This will serve as the main tracking point for your beer's fermentation process.

**Logging Fermentation**

To log your beer's fermentation, follow these steps:

1. Open the app and tap on the "Fermenter" tab.
2. Select the current day or date to record your measurements.
3. Take a reading of the specific gravity (SG) using a hydrometer or refractometer. Tap "Log Reading" to input the value.
4. Choose from pre-set conversion formulas to convert the SG reading to ABV, IBU, and gravity units.
5. Select any relevant notes or observations about your batch, such as temperature or yeast activity.

**Temperature Monitoring**

Barmwatch allows you to set temperature alerts for your fermenter. To do this:

1. Tap on the "Fermenter" tab and select the current day or date.
2. Tap the "+" icon at the top-right corner of the screen to add a new note.
3. Select "Temperature Alert" from the dropdown menu.
4. Set the desired temperature range (e.g., 15°C - 20°C) and tap "Save".

**Exporting Your Brew Log**

To export your brew log as a PDF, follow these steps:

1. Tap on the "Brew Log" tab at the bottom of the screen.
2. Select the date range you'd like to export (e.g., all readings since the last update).
3. Choose the desired format for your log: summary, detailed, or PDF.

**Tips and Tricks**

* Use Barmwatch's built-in temperature data logging feature to track temperature fluctuations during fermentation.
* Take multiple readings on each day of fermentation to get an accurate picture of your batch's progress.
* Keep a record of any batches that didn't quite go as planned – it can be helpful for troubleshooting future brews.

**Conclusion**

Barmwatch is a valuable tool for home brewers, providing a structured way to track and monitor the brewing process. By following these steps, you'll be able to log your beer's fermentation, set temperature alerts, and export a brew log as a PDF. Happy brewing!
