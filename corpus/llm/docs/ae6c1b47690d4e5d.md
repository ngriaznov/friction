### Beginner’s Guide: Using Barmwatch to Track Your Homebrew

Welcome to the exciting world of home brewing! One key aspect of crafting your perfect batch is monitoring fermentation conditions accurately. Enter **Barmwatch**, an intuitive mobile app that helps you log batches, monitor temperature, and keep a detailed brew log. In this tutorial, we'll walk you through setting up Barmwatch for tracking your next batch.

#### Step 1: Downloading and Setting Up Barmwatch

First, download the Barmwatch app from either the App Store or Google Play Store. Once installed, open it to get started. You’ll need to create a new account if you don’t have one already. Follow these steps:

1. **Sign In/Up**: If you’re new, tap "Get Started" and follow the prompts to sign up using your email address. For returning users, simply log in.
2. **Create a New Batch**:
   - Tap on “Batches” at the bottom of the screen.
   - Select “+ Add New Batch.”
   - Enter basic details like batch name, date started, and style (e.g., Ale, Lager).

#### Step 2: Logging Temperature Data

Tracking fermentation temperature is crucial for ensuring a smooth brewing process. Barmwatch makes this easy.

1. **Set Up Your Thermometer**:
   - Ensure you have a compatible thermometer. Most modern digital thermometers can be paired with the app.
   - Follow your thermometer’s instructions to pair it with Barmwatch (this usually involves tapping “Add Device” and then selecting your model).

2. **Start Logging Temperatures**:
   - Tap on the batch you just created.
   - You’ll see a calendar icon; tap this to start logging temperatures.
   - Place your thermometer in the fermenter, wait for it to settle (this might take a few minutes), and then tap “Log” at the top of the screen.
   - Barmwatch will automatically log the temperature. If you want to add notes or other data, like specific gravity readings, do so now.

#### Step 3: Setting Temperature Alerts

Barmwatch allows you to set up alerts for temperature deviations that could affect your fermentation process.

1. **Access Alert Settings**:
   - Go back to the batch screen.
   - Tap on “Alerts” from the menu on the left side of the screen (it looks like a bell icon).
   
2. **Configure Alerts**:
   - Set up alerts by specifying temperature thresholds. For example, you might want to be notified if the temperature rises above 75°F or falls below 60°F.
   - Tap “Add Alert” and configure your settings.

3. **Receive Notifications**:
   - Once set, Barmwatch will notify you via push notifications on your phone whenever these thresholds are breached.
   - Adjust alerts as needed based on the specific needs of your brew.

#### Step 4: Exporting Your Brew Log

Keeping detailed logs is essential for learning and improving over time. You can export your brew log to share with fellow brewers or keep records.

1. **Export Batch Details**:
   - From the batch screen, tap “More” (three dots) at the top right.
   - Select “Export Batch” from the menu that appears.
   
2. **Choose Export Format**:
   - Barmwatch supports exporting as a PDF, which is great for printing or sharing.
   - Tap on “PDF” to generate your brew log.

3. **Save and Share**:
   - Once exported, you can save the file directly to your device’s documents folder.
   - From there, you can share it via email, cloud storage services like Dropbox, or print it out for reference.

#### Additional Tips

- **Regular Updates**: Keep Barmwatch updated to ensure you have the latest features and bug fixes.
- **Backup Your Data**: Regularly backup your brew logs using the app’s export feature. This ensures that all your hard work is protected.
- **Community Support**: Join the Barmwatch community for tips, troubleshooting, and inspiration from other home brewers.

By following these steps, you’ll be well on your way to effectively using Barmwatch as a powerful tool in your brewing arsenal. Happy brewing!
