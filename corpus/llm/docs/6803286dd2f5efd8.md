##  MoltenPath Firmware Troubleshooting Guide

This guide addresses three common issues encountered with MoltenPath firmware: layer shifting, nozzle clogs, and bed adhesion failures. Follow the troubleshooting steps provided to diagnose and resolve these problems. 

**1. Layer Shifting Mid-Print**

Layer shifting occurs when your print starts deviating from its intended path mid-print, resulting in a visually flawed and structurally compromised model.

**Diagnostic Steps:**

* **Visual Inspection:** Examine your printed object for noticeable gaps or misalignment between layers. Identify where the shift first appears.
* **Filament Path Analysis:** Analyze the sliced gcode file (your 3D printing instructions) using software like Cura or PrusaSlicer. Look for any sudden changes in direction or movement that might indicate a potential issue with the G-code itself.
* **Firmware Version Check:** Ensure you are running the latest version of MoltenPath firmware. Older versions may contain bugs that contribute to layer shifting.

**Fixes:**

* **Tighten Belt Connections:** Loose belts can cause inaccurate movement, leading to layer shifts.  Inspect and tighten all belt connections on your printer's X, Y, and Z axes. Refer to your printer's manual for specific tightening procedures.
* **Calibrate Steps per Unit:** Miscalibrated steps per unit settings in the firmware can lead to incorrect movements. Recalibrate these values by using a known distance and measuring the actual movement of your printer. Adjust the steps per unit accordingly in the MoltenPath firmware settings.
* **Check Extruder Motor Alignment:** Ensure the extruder motor is properly aligned with the filament drive gear. Misalignment can cause inconsistent extrusion, leading to layer shifting. 
* **Firmware Update:** If you suspect a bug in your current firmware version, update to the latest release from the official MoltenPath website.

**2. Nozzle Clogs**

Nozzle clogs occur when melted plastic gets stuck inside the nozzle, disrupting the printing process and producing inconsistent extrusion.

**Diagnostic Steps:**

* **Extrusion Test:** Conduct a short print test with your chosen filament. Observe if the filament extrudes smoothly and consistently. If you notice gaps or uneven flow, it indicates a potential clog.
* **Visual Inspection:** Carefully examine the nozzle tip for any visible blockages using a magnifying glass or flashlight. 
* **Filament Type:** Consider the type of filament you are using. Abrasive filaments (like wood-filled) can be more prone to clogging.

**Fixes:**

* **Heat and Cleaning:** Heat the nozzle to its highest recommended temperature. Gently run a cleaning wire through the nozzle to dislodge any obstructions. Once cooled, flush the nozzle with hot water or acetone for further cleaning.
* **Filament Change:** Switch to a different filament type that is less prone to clogging. Consider using higher-quality filaments known for smoother extrusion.
* **Nozzle Replacement:** If the clog persists despite cleaning attempts, consider replacing the nozzle altogether. 

**3. Bed Adhesion Failures**

Bed adhesion failures occur when the printed object detaches from the print bed during printing, leading to incomplete or ruined prints.

**Diagnostic Steps:**

* **Visual Inspection:** Examine the print bed surface for any debris, scratches, or unevenness that might hinder adhesion.
* **Print Surface Condition:** Check the type of print surface you are using (e.g., glass, PEI sheet). Ensure it is clean and free of any residues or oils.
* **Bed Leveling Accuracy:** Poorly leveled bed can cause inconsistent contact between the nozzle and the printing surface, leading to weak adhesion.

**Fixes:**

* **Surface Cleaning:** Thoroughly clean the print bed surface with alcohol or a dedicated cleaning solution. Ensure it is completely dry before resuming printing.
* **Adhesion Promoters:** Apply a thin layer of adhesive spray or hairspray to the print bed to improve initial adhesion. Refer to your specific filament type for recommended adhesives.
* **Bed Temperature Adjustment:** Experiment with different bed temperatures within the recommended range for your filament type. Increasing the bed temperature can enhance adhesion in some cases. 
* **Bed Leveling Calibration:** Carefully calibrate your printer's bed leveling settings using a feeler gauge or automated leveling procedure.


By following these troubleshooting steps and utilizing MoltenPath's capabilities, you can effectively diagnose and resolve common 3D printing issues, ensuring successful and high-quality prints.
