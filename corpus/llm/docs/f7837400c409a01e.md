## Level Up Your Brew: Tracking Your Beer with Barmwatch

So you're brewing – fantastic! But keeping track of your fermentation process can be tricky. It’s easy to lose sight of crucial temperature data, which directly impacts the flavor and quality of your beer. That’s where apps like Barmwatch come in. This tutorial will walk you through using Barmwatch to meticulously log your brew, set alerts, and even export a professional-looking brew log as a PDF.

**What is Barmwatch?**

Barmwatch is a mobile app (available on iOS and Android) specifically designed for homebrewers to track fermentation temperatures and record vital brewing information. It’s incredibly user-friendly and can significantly improve your brewing consistency. 


**Getting Started with Barmwatch:**

1. **Download & Setup:** First, download Barmwatch from the App Store (iOS) or Google Play Store (Android).  Create an account – this allows you to sync your data across devices and back it up.
2. **Adding Your Batch:** Tap the "+" button on the main screen to start a new batch. You’ll be prompted to enter key details:
   * **Batch Name:** Give your beer a descriptive name (e.g., “IPA – First Brew”).
   * **Grain Bill:** Enter the weight of each grain addition. Barmwatch will automatically calculate the original gravity (OG) based on these inputs. 
   * **Yeast:** Select your yeast strain from the database, or add a new one if needed.
   * **Target OG & FG:**  Enter your desired Original Gravity and Final Gravity – this is crucial for calculating ABV!
   * **Brew Date:** Set the date you started brewing.


**Tracking Fermentation with Temperature Alerts:**

This is where Barmwatch really shines. 

1. **Connecting Your Thermometer:** Barmwatch works best when connected to a digital thermometer that transmits data wirelessly (Bluetooth). Ensure your thermometer is compatible and properly paired within the app’s settings.
2. **Starting the Fermentation:** Once you've entered your batch details, tap "Start Fermentation." The app will begin tracking the temperature of your fermenter.
3. **Setting Temperature Alerts:** This is key! Tap the “Alerts” tab. Here, you can set custom temperature alerts:
   * **High Temperature Alert:**  Set a threshold (e.g., 84°F / 29°C) – Barmwatch will send you a notification if your fermentation gets too hot, which can lead to off-flavors.
   * **Low Temperature Alert:** Set a lower threshold (e.g., 65°F / 18°C) -  Too cold and yeast activity slows down dramatically, potentially impacting the final beer flavor.
4. **Real-Time Monitoring:** The app displays your fermentation temperature in real-time on the main screen. You can see how quickly it’s rising or falling.



**Logging Notes & Adjustments:**

* Throughout the fermentation process, you can tap the “Notes” tab to record observations – things like changes in color, activity levels, or any adjustments you make (e.g., adding a small amount of priming sugar).


**Exporting Your Brew Log as a PDF:**

When your fermentation is complete and you’ve reached your target final gravity, it's time to document everything!

1. **Tap the Batch:** Select the batch you want to export from the main screen.
2. **Tap “Export”:** This will generate a detailed brew log including all entered data, temperature readings, and notes.
3. **Choose PDF Format:**  Barmwatch allows you to save your log directly as a PDF file for easy sharing or archiving. 


**Tips & Tricks:**

* **Accuracy is Key:** Ensure your thermometer is accurate – calibration is important!
* **Regular Logging:** Log temperature readings at least every hour, especially during the initial stages of fermentation.
* **Experiment and Learn:** Barmwatch isn’t just a tracking tool; it's a learning tool. Analyze your brew logs to understand how temperature impacts your beer.

With Barmwatch, you can transform your brewing from an art based on intuition into a science-backed process, leading to consistently delicious homebrews!  Happy brewing!
