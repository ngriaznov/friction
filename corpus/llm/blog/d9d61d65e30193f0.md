**Cutting Cloud Costs: How We Reduced Our AWS Bill by $5,000 per Month**

As a development team, we're no strangers to the cloud. We've been using Amazon Web Services (AWS) for years to host our applications and infrastructure. However, like many teams, we've faced growing pains as our usage has increased over time. Our monthly AWS bill had become unsustainable, and it was clear that something needed to change.

After conducting a thorough review of our resources, we identified several opportunities to optimize our cloud costs. In this post, we'll walk through the steps we took to cut our AWS bill by roughly 50% – from $10,000 per month to just under $5,000.

**The Problem: Oversized EC2 Instances**

One of the primary contributors to our high AWS bill was our use of oversized EC2 instances. We had a mix of small, medium, and large instances scattered throughout our environment, many of which were running well below capacity. To put this into perspective, one of our larger instances was costing us $1,200 per month, but it only used about 20% of its allocated resources.

We realized that we could achieve significant cost savings by rightsizing these instances to more modest sizes. We started by identifying the instances that were underutilized and replaced them with smaller counterparts. For example, instead of running a large instance costing $1,200 per month, we upgraded to two medium-sized instances costing $600 each – a total savings of $480.

**The Solution: Spot Instances for Batch Jobs**

Another major contributor to our high AWS bill was our use of on-demand EC2 instances for batch jobs. These jobs typically ran for short periods, but they were still being billed at the same rate as full-time production instances. We decided to switch these batch jobs over to spot instances, which offer a discounted rate in exchange for potentially variable performance.

Spot instances are ideal for non-critical workloads like batch processing, where downtime is acceptable and the primary concern is cost. By using spot instances, we were able to reduce our costs by up to 90% for certain batch jobs. For example, one job that previously cost us $1,000 per month now costs just $100 – a savings of $900.

**The Results: A 50% Reduction in AWS Costs**

After implementing these changes, we were thrilled to see the impact on our AWS bill. Our total monthly costs dropped from $10,000 to around $5,000 – a reduction of roughly 50%. While there are still opportunities for further optimization, this result was a major milestone for our team.

**Takeaways and Best Practices**

As we reflect on our experience, here are some key takeaways and best practices that can help other teams optimize their AWS costs:

1.  **Rightsizing is key**: Regularly review your EC2 instance sizes to ensure they're aligned with your actual usage.
2.  **Spot instances for batch jobs**: Consider using spot instances for non-critical workloads like batch processing, which can lead to significant cost savings.
3.  **Monitor and adjust**: Continuously monitor your AWS costs and adjust your strategy as needed to optimize performance and reduce costs.

**Conclusion**

Cutting our AWS bill by $5,000 per month has been a game-changer for our team. We've reduced waste, optimized resources, and achieved significant cost savings – all while maintaining the reliability and scalability that we need from the cloud. If you're struggling with high AWS costs, we hope this post has provided valuable insights to help you get started on your own optimization journey.

We'll continue to monitor and refine our strategy as our usage evolves, but for now, we're proud of what we've accomplished – and excited to see where this newfound cost savings will take us!
