# Cutting Costs with Smart Cloud Optimization: A Small Team's Journey

In today’s digital age, cloud services like Amazon Web Services (AWS) have become an indispensable part of many businesses' tech stacks. However, the cost associated with these services can quickly add up, especially for small teams on tight budgets. In this blog post, we'll share a real-world case study from a small team that managed to cut its monthly AWS bill roughly in half by implementing some strategic changes. We’ll explore how they rightsized their EC2 instances and switched several batch jobs to spot instances, providing you with actionable insights for your own cloud optimization efforts.

## The Initial Situation

Our team started off with an AWS bill of around $10,000 per month. This included a mix of on-demand and reserved instances across various services like EC2, S3, and RDS. While the initial setup was necessary to support their growing workload, it wasn't optimized for cost-effectiveness.

### Key Services and Usage
- **EC2 Instances:** Primarily used for development environments and occasional batch processing.
- **S3 Storage:** Used for storing backups and static content.
- **RDS Databases:** Managed relational databases for critical applications.

## The Optimization Journey

### 1. Rightsizing EC2 Instances

The first step in our optimization journey was to review the usage patterns of their EC2 instances. By analyzing the CPU, memory, and network utilization over a period of time, they identified several underutilized instances that were running at full capacity only during specific times.

#### Before
- **On-Demand Instances:** 10 large instances (4 vCPUs, 32 GB RAM) running 24/7.
- **Reserved Instances:** 5 medium instances (2 vCPUs, 8 GB RAM) for critical applications.

#### After
- **Rightsized Instances:** 6 small instances (1 vCPU, 2 GB RAM) during off-peak hours and 3 medium instances (2 vCPUs, 4 GB RAM) during peak times.
- **Reserved Instances:** 5 medium instances (2 vCPUs, 8 GB RAM) for critical applications.

By rightsizing their EC2 instances, the team reduced their overall instance count by 40% while maintaining the same level of performance. This change alone saved them approximately $1,500 per month.

### 2. Switching to Spot Instances

The next step was to leverage AWS Spot Instances for batch processing jobs that didn't require high availability or strict SLAs. Spot Instances are significantly cheaper than on-demand instances and can be terminated at any time by Amazon, making them ideal for non-critical workloads.

#### Before
- **Batch Jobs:** Run on 2 large on-demand EC2 instances.

#### After
- **Batch Jobs:** Run on 4 small spot instances (1 vCPU, 2 GB RAM).

By switching to Spot Instances, the team was able to reduce their batch job costs by about $800 per month. The total savings from this change alone amounted to approximately $2,300 per month.

### 3. Optimizing S3 Storage

While not as significant in terms of cost reduction, optimizing S3 storage also played a role in the overall savings. By implementing lifecycle policies and using S3 Intelligent-Tiering, they ensured that less frequently accessed data was stored at lower costs.

#### Before
- **S3 Usage:** No lifecycle policies or tiered storage.

#### After
- **S3 Lifecycle Policies:** Set to move older data to cheaper storage classes.
- **S3 Intelligent-Tiering:** Used for automatic cost optimization based on access patterns.

This change resulted in a small but noticeable reduction in S3 costs, contributing an additional $200 per month to the overall savings.

### 4. Monitoring and Automation

Finally, implementing monitoring and automation tools helped the team stay on top of their AWS usage and ensure that they continued to optimize their resources over time.

- **AWS Budgets:** Set up budgets to alert them when costs exceeded certain thresholds.
- **CloudWatch Alarms:** Configured alarms for resource utilization to prevent unnecessary scaling.
- **Automation Scripts:** Created scripts to automatically resize instances based on real-time demand.

These tools helped the team maintain a proactive approach to cost management, ensuring that they didn't fall back into old habits and continued to benefit from their optimizations.

## The Final Result

By implementing these changes, our small team was able to reduce its monthly AWS bill by approximately $4,000, or roughly 50%. This significant reduction not only freed up budget for other critical areas of the business but also provided a valuable lesson in the importance of regular cloud optimization.

## Conclusion

For any small team looking to optimize their AWS costs without sacrificing performance, this case study offers several key takeaways:

1. **Rightsizing EC2 Instances:** Analyze usage patterns and adjust instance sizes accordingly.
2. **Leverage Spot Instances:** Use them for non-critical workloads to save on costs.
3. **Optimize Storage Costs:** Implement lifecycle policies and tiered storage solutions.
4. **Monitor and Automate:** Use tools like AWS Budgets, CloudWatch Alarms, and automation scripts to stay proactive.

By following these steps, you can achieve significant cost savings while maintaining the performance and reliability of your cloud infrastructure.
