**Title: From Gerrit to GitHub Pull Requests: A Five-Person Engineering Team’s Journey**

In the ever-evolving world of software development, choosing the right tools for collaboration and code review is crucial. Our five-person engineering team at TechSolutions Inc., a mid-sized startup specializing in cloud-based solutions, recently made a strategic shift from Gerrit to GitHub pull requests. This transition was not merely a change in tool but a reflection of our growing needs, challenges, and the evolving dynamics of our project management style. In this blog post, we’ll delve into what we missed about Gerrit, what became easier with GitHub, and why this switch ultimately benefitted our workflow.

**What We Missed About Gerrit**

Gerrit, with its robust integration capabilities and detailed change history, was a staple in our development process for several reasons. Firstly, the granular control over code changes that Gerrit offered was unparalleled. Each commit could be reviewed line by line, which is invaluable when working on complex systems where subtle errors can lead to significant issues down the line. Additionally, the ability to tag and group related changes (change IDs) made it easier for us to track dependencies across large projects.

Another aspect we valued about Gerrit was its integration with our continuous integration (CI) server. This seamless connection ensured that any code change could be automatically tested against our suite of unit tests and other validation scripts, reducing the risk of introducing bugs into our production environment. The feedback loop between Gerrit’s review process and CI testing was a cornerstone of our development methodology.

**What Became Easier With GitHub Pull Requests**

While we cherished the precision and integration capabilities of Gerrit, several aspects of our workflow with GitHub pull requests (PRs) made our lives significantly easier:

1. **Accessibility and Simplicity:** GitHub’s interface is more user-friendly and accessible to both technical and non-technical team members. This democratization of access means that anyone on the team can submit a PR, ask for reviews, or provide feedback without needing deep technical expertise. It streamlined communication within our small team, making it easier for everyone to contribute meaningfully.

2. **Integration with GitHub Issues:** One of the standout features of GitHub is its tight integration with issues and project management tools like Trello or Jira. This means that when a PR is created, it can be linked directly to specific tasks or bugs in our issue tracker. This linkage ensures that discussions around code changes are always tied back to their intended purpose, making it easier for developers to understand the context of each change.

3. **Community and Collaboration:** GitHub’s platform encourages collaboration through comments on PRs, which often lead to more interactive conversations among team members. The ability to comment directly on lines of code within a PR has made discussions about changes much clearer and less ambiguous than in Gerrit's patch format. This feature fosters a culture of learning and knowledge sharing within the team.

4. **Speed of Deployment:** GitHub’s CI integration, powered by services like Travis CI or CircleCI, allows for faster feedback loops on our code changes. We can now see whether a PR passes all tests almost immediately after submission, which reduces waiting times and speeds up our deployment process. This agility is crucial in an industry where time-to-market can make or break projects.

5. **Open Source Contributions:** As part of our development philosophy, we often look to contribute back to the open-source community. GitHub’s ease of submitting pull requests for external repositories has made this possible without a steep learning curve. It’s encouraged a culture of giving back and collaboration within TechSolutions Inc., further enriching our technical capabilities.

**The Decision Behind the Switch**

Our decision to switch from Gerrit to GitHub pull requests was driven by several factors: the need for more accessible communication, improved integration with project management tools, and the desire to foster an environment of rapid iteration and learning. While Gerrit offered unmatched precision in code review, the evolving needs of our team—particularly regarding collaboration across diverse skill levels—made GitHub a better fit.

**Conclusion**

The transition from Gerrit to GitHub pull requests was not without its challenges but has proven to be a rewarding shift for TechSolutions Inc. Our engineering team now enjoys a more collaborative, accessible, and efficient workflow that aligns with our goals of rapid development, high-quality code, and community engagement. This move underscores the importance of choosing tools that grow with your project needs and foster an inclusive environment where every voice can contribute to success.

In embracing GitHub, we’ve not only streamlined our process but also strengthened our team’s cohesion and productivity. As we continue to innovate in the cloud solutions space, we remain open to evolving our toolset further, always prioritizing what best serves our development goals and values.
