Subject: Migration of Internal Wiki from Confluence to MkDocs

Dear Team,

As we continue to grow and evolve as a company, it's essential that our internal tools and infrastructure adapt to meet our changing needs. After careful consideration, I'm excited to announce that we'll be migrating our internal wiki from Confluence to a self-hosted MkDocs setup.

The main reasons for this migration are:

* Licensing costs: With the increasing popularity of open-source projects, licensing fees for proprietary platforms like Confluence have become prohibitively expensive.
* Search quality: While Confluence's search functionality has improved over time, it still can't match the ease and accuracy of a self-hosted search solution.
* Git-based review: MkDocs allows us to leverage our existing version control system (Git), making it easier for team members to contribute and review changes.

Over the next six weeks, we'll be performing a phased migration. Here's an overview of what you can expect:

Weeks 1-2: Migration preparation

* Existing Confluence pages will be exported as Markdown files.
* MkDocs setup will be configured on our server.

Weeks 3-4: Content migration

* Individual contributors will need to recreate their existing pages in the new MkDocs setup. Don't worry; we'll provide guidance and support during this process.
* If you have any concerns or questions, please reach out to [your name] (our wiki administrator).

Weeks 5-6: Testing and deployment

* We'll conduct thorough testing of the new wiki setup to ensure it meets our requirements.
* Once testing is complete, we'll deploy the new platform.

During this migration period, please note the following:

* Existing links to Confluence pages will be updated automatically. However, if you encounter any issues, feel free to reach out for assistance.
* If you have any existing plugins or integrations with Confluence (e.g., integration with our project management tool), we'll need to review and update these accordingly.

To ensure a smooth transition, I recommend the following:

* Review your existing pages and consider revising them for better organization and formatting in MkDocs.
* Familiarize yourself with MkDocs' syntax and features to make it easier to contribute to the wiki.

If you have any questions or concerns about this migration, please don't hesitate to reach out. I'll be hosting regular Q&A sessions during Week 3-4; check your emails for more information on dates and times.

I'm excited about this new chapter in our company's journey, and I'm confident that the self-hosted MkDocs setup will bring numerous benefits to our team. Thank you for your cooperation and understanding as we embark on this migration process.
