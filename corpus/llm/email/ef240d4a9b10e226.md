### The Wood Selection Process

When choosing the wood for this project, I was drawn to the rich, warm tones of walnut. I visited several local lumberyards and online suppliers to find the perfect boards. After much deliberation, I settled on a piece that had a beautiful grain pattern with some subtle figuring. It was about 3 inches thick, which provided enough material for the top while ensuring it wouldn’t be too heavy.

### The Build Process

The build itself took me approximately 120 hours over several months. Here’s a quick rundown of the key steps:

- **Design and Layout**: I spent quite some time designing the table to ensure it would fit well in my dining room.
- **Cutting and Sanding**: Once the design was finalized, I cut the pieces and sanded them down to a smooth finish.
- **Glue-Up**: This is where things got interesting. I had planned for a perfect assembly, but during the glue-up process, one of the legs didn’t align properly due to a slight misalignment in the cutting. To fix this, I had to carefully remove some material from the leg and re-plane it before gluing everything together again.

### The Mistake and Its Fix

The mistake with the leg was a bit frustrating but ultimately taught me an important lesson about double-checking measurements. Once I realized the misalignment, I took the time to carefully plane down the leg until it fit perfectly. This step added a couple of hours to my build time but ensured that the table would be structurally sound and aesthetically pleasing.

### The Finish

For the finish, I opted for a natural oil finish using Tung Oil. It’s a versatile product that enhances the wood's natural beauty while providing a protective layer. I applied three coats over several days to ensure even coverage and a smooth surface. The result is a table that not only looks beautiful but also feels comfortable under my hands.

### Total Build Time

From start to finish, including all the time spent on design, cutting, sanding, glue-up, and finishing, the project took around 120 hours. While it was a significant investment of time, I found each step rewarding as I watched the table come together piece by piece.
