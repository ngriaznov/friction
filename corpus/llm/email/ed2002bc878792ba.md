Subject: Exciting News: Moving Our Documentation to MkDocs!

Hi Team,

I’m excited to announce a significant upgrade to our internal documentation process – we're migrating from Confluence to a self-hosted MkDocs setup! This is a strategic move designed to improve our knowledge sharing and overall engineering efficiency.

For quite some time, the ongoing licensing costs associated with Confluence have been a growing concern. Beyond that, we’ve identified areas where Confluence's search functionality and content organization haven't always met our needs, particularly for quickly finding specific technical information.  Finally, we want to leverage a more modern, version-controlled approach to documentation – something that aligns perfectly with our development workflows.

MkDocs is a fantastic static site generator built on Python, offering significant advantages: it’s free and open source, boasts significantly improved search capabilities, and most importantly, integrates seamlessly with Git for version control and collaborative review. This means we can track changes, easily revert to previous versions, and ensure everyone is working from the latest documentation – just like our code!
