Subject: Important Update: Migration of Our Internal Wiki from Confluence to MkDocs

Dear Team,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to inform you about an important change regarding our internal documentation platform. Effective immediately, we will be migrating our current wiki hosted on Confluence to a self-hosted MkDocs setup. This transition is driven by several strategic considerations aimed at enhancing our workflow efficiency and accessibility.

**Reasoning Behind the Migration:**

1. **Licensing Cost:** The primary driver for this change is the reduction in licensing costs associated with Confluence. By adopting an open-source solution like MkDocs, we can significantly lower our operational expenses while maintaining a high standard of documentation quality.

2. **Search Quality:** MkDocs offers superior search capabilities compared to Confluence. This improvement will enable faster and more accurate retrieval of information, which is crucial for our development processes and knowledge sharing across the organization.

3. **Git-Based Review Process:** Integrating MkDocs with Git allows us to leverage a version control system that supports a more robust review process for documentation changes. This ensures better collaboration among team members and maintains a clear history of document modifications.

**Migration Timeline:**

We have planned this migration over the next six weeks to ensure minimal disruption to our daily operations. Here’s a brief overview of the timeline:

- **Weeks 1-2:** Preparation phase, including setting up the MkDocs environment, migrating templates, and establishing backup procedures for existing Confluence pages.
  
- **Weeks 3-4:** Gradual migration of content from Confluence to MkDocs. During this period, we will work on mapping existing pages to their new counterparts in MkDocs.

- **Weeks 5-6:** Final adjustments, testing, and user training sessions to ensure a smooth transition for all users.

**What You Need to Do:**

As individual contributors, you will need to update your existing wiki pages to the new MkDocs format. We encourage you to begin this process as soon as possible to avoid any gaps in documentation availability. Our technical team will be available to assist with the migration of content and provide guidance on how to structure your pages for optimal use within MkDocs.

**Contact Information:**

If you have any questions, concerns, or need assistance during the transition, please reach out to our dedicated support channel:

- **Support Email:** wiki-migration-support@company.com
- **Slack Channel:** #wiki-migration-help

Our team is committed to ensuring a seamless migration and will be available to address any issues that arise. We appreciate your patience and cooperation as we work through this transition together.
