Subject: Finished My Walnut Dining Table – A Journey Through Wood & Mistakes

Hi Fellow Makers,

I hope this email finds you well and inspired by your own projects! I’m excited to share my recent experience building a walnut dining table, which has been both challenging and rewarding. Let me walk you through the process—from wood selection to finishing touches—and include some lessons learned along the way.

**Wood Selection Process:**
Choosing the right wood was crucial for this project. I opted for high-quality domestic walnut due to its rich color and straight grain, which would complement my dining room’s decor beautifully. At the lumberyard, I carefully inspected each board for knots and defects. I selected pieces with minimal imperfections, ensuring they were free from cracks or warping. After purchasing about 15 linear feet of walnut, I prepped the wood by sanding it down to a smooth finish, which set the stage for a flawless assembly.

**Building Process & Mistakes:**
The build itself was quite straightforward, but one unexpected hiccup occurred during the glue-up phase. While clamping the legs and tabletop together, I noticed that one of the legs had become slightly warped due to improper storage before shipping. This warp caused misalignment when glued, leading to gaps between the leg and the table top. To resolve this issue, I carefully removed the glue from the affected area, re-planed the leg using a hand plane for precision, and then re-glued it securely. It was a bit of a setback, but it taught me the importance of inspecting every piece thoroughly before assembly.

**Finish Used:**
For finishing my walnut dining table, I decided on a natural oil finish to preserve its rich tones while providing protection against everyday wear. After applying several coats of tung oil, which allowed each layer to dry completely between applications, I polished it with fine steel wool for that smooth, lustrous look. This finish not only enhances the wood’s beauty but also makes maintenance easier over time.

**Total Build Time:**
From start to finish, the entire build took approximately 10 weeks. This includes time spent on planning, sourcing materials, and allowing each stage of assembly and finishing to dry properly. While this might seem like a lengthy process, it allowed me to focus on quality rather than rushing through steps. I believe patience is key in woodworking, especially when working with premium materials like walnut.
