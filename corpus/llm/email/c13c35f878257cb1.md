Subject: Migration of Internal Wiki from Confluence to MkDocs

Dear Team,

I hope this message finds you well. I am writing to inform you about an upcoming change in our internal documentation system. As part of our ongoing efforts to enhance efficiency and reduce costs, we are transitioning from using Confluence to a self-hosted MkDocs setup.

### Reasoning for the Migration
1. **Licensing Cost**: Confluence's licensing model can be quite expensive, especially as our team grows. By moving to a self-hosted solution like MkDocs, we will significantly reduce these costs.
2. **Search Quality**: MkDocs offers advanced search capabilities that are more user-friendly and efficient compared to the current system. This will improve our ability to find relevant information quickly.
3. **Git-Based Review**: With MkDocs, changes can be tracked using Git, allowing for a transparent review process where team members can collaborate on updates before they go live.

### Migration Timeline
The migration is scheduled over the next six weeks and will proceed as follows:

- **Week 1-2**: Preparation phase – setting up the new server environment.
- **Week 3-4**: Data migration – transferring existing content from Confluence to MkDocs. During this period, we may experience some downtime or reduced functionality.
- **Week 5-6**: Testing and final adjustments – ensuring everything works smoothly before going live.

### What You Need to Do
1. **Backup Your Content**: Before the transition begins, please ensure you have a backup of any important pages you’ve created in Confluence. We will provide instructions on how to export your content.
2. **Update Your Pages**: Once we start the migration process, you may need to update or reformat some of your existing content for it to work with MkDocs. We will provide templates and guidelines to make this easier.
3. **Contribute to New Content**: Start creating new pages in the new system as needed. This is a great opportunity to improve documentation and share knowledge.
