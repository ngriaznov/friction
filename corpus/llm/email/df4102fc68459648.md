Subject: Upcoming Wiki Migration from Confluence to MkDocs

Dear Team,

As you're aware, our internal wiki has been hosted on Confluence for some time now. After reviewing the current setup and exploring alternative solutions, we've made the decision to migrate our wiki to a self-hosted MkDocs instance over the next six weeks.

The primary drivers behind this change are licensing costs and search quality. Our usage of Confluence exceeds the standard enterprise license, resulting in significant additional fees. Furthermore, while Confluence provides robust features for large-scale corporate wikis, its search capabilities have become increasingly cumbersome as our knowledge base has grown. MkDocs offers a more agile solution that leverages GitHub's robust search functionality and version control.

Another key benefit of this change is the integration with Git-based review processes. As you know, we're adopting GitHub as our primary code repository. With MkDocs, we can leverage these same workflows to manage our documentation, ensuring consistency and quality across both code and documentation.

Here's an overview of what you can expect during the migration process:

* Week 1-2: Content inventory and metadata cleanup
	+ We'll be reviewing all existing wiki pages to ensure accurate categorization and tagging.
	+ Please refrain from making any new content changes during this period, as we're trying to capture a snapshot of our current state.
* Week 3-4: Migration to MkDocs
	+ Our team will transfer the relevant data and settings over to the self-hosted MkDocs setup.
	+ You'll be able to access the new wiki instance for testing and feedback purposes.
* Week 5-6: Finalize metadata, testing, and launch

For individual contributors, here's what you need to do with your existing pages:

* Review your content and ensure it remains accurate and up-to-date. If necessary, update the information or create a corresponding issue in GitHub for our documentation team to address.
* As we're not migrating any Confluence-specific formatting or macros, please remove these elements from your pages.

If you have questions about the migration process or need assistance with updating your content, please don't hesitate to reach out. You can contact me directly, and I'll ensure that the relevant team members are informed.

This change will bring numerous benefits in terms of cost savings, improved search capabilities, and integration with our existing GitHub workflows. We appreciate your understanding as we make this transition over the next six weeks. Your patience and cooperation are invaluable to a successful migration.

Thank you for your continued hard work and commitment to maintaining accurate documentation.
